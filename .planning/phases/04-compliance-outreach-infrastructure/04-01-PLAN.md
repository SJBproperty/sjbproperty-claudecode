# Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)
## SJB Property Group Ltd — B2B Landlord Marketing

### Document Control
- Version: 1.0
- Date: {today}
- Data controller: SJB Property Group Ltd
- ICO registration: [reference number — already current]

### Part 1: Purpose Test
- Legitimate interest: Direct marketing of property management services (BTL management at 10% fee, R2R guaranteed rent for HMO landlords) to landlords of rental properties in Greater Manchester
- Who benefits: SJB (business development) and landlords (professional management reducing compliance burden, void periods, tenant hassle)
- Is the interest real and present: Yes — active pipeline with 9,545 landlord records from public data sources
- Legal basis: UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests. UK Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 explicitly confirms direct marketing as a legitimate interest

### Part 2: Necessity Test
- Is processing necessary: Yes — cannot identify and contact landlords with compliance issues without processing their property and contact data
- Less intrusive alternative: No — data sources are already public (EPC Register, Companies House, Land Registry CCOD, council HMO registers, OpenRent listings). Processing is minimal (name, entity type, property address, EPC rating, email if corporate)
- Data minimisation: Only process name, entity type, company number, property address, EPC rating, contact details. No sensitive personal data processed.

### Part 3: Balancing Test
- Nature of data: Business contact information from publicly available government registers and property listing platforms
- Data sources: EPC Register (public government API), Companies House (public register), Land Registry CCOD (public dataset), Council HMO licensing registers (public/FOI), OpenRent (public property listings)
- Reasonable expectations: Landlords who list properties on public registers and letting platforms expect to receive business correspondence about property services
- Relationship: No prior relationship — cold outreach. However, communications are relevant to their role as property landlords
- Impact on individuals: Low — B2B context, property management services directly relevant to their business activity
- Safeguards in place:
  - Opt-out mechanism on all channels ("Reply STOP", return address on mail)
  - Universal suppression list — opt-out on one channel suppresses all channels
  - PECR compliance — email only to Ltd/LLP corporate subscribers; sole traders receive direct mail and phone only
  - 12-month data retention with review — delete if no engagement
  - Data stored securely in local SQLite database, not shared with third parties
- Children or vulnerable people: No — B2B landlord data only
- Objection mechanism: Landlords can object at any time via email (sam@sjbproperty.com), reply STOP, or return mail. Objection results in immediate universal suppression.

### Conclusion
The legitimate interest in direct marketing of property management services to landlords outweighs the minimal privacy impact, given the data is sourced from public registers, the communications are commercially relevant, and robust opt-out and suppression mechanisms are in place.
```

**1. privacy-notice.md** — Following ICO Article 13/14 guidance. CRITICAL: This uses Article 14 (data NOT obtained directly from the data subject), which requires listing sources of data.

Required sections and content:

```
# Privacy Notice — Landlord Lead Data
## SJB Property Group Ltd

### Who we are
Data controller: SJB Property Group Ltd
Contact: sam@sjbproperty.com
ICO registration: [reference number]
Website: sjbpropertygroup.com

### What data we collect
- Full name or company name
- Entity type (Ltd, LLP, sole trader, individual)
- Company number (if applicable)
- Property address(es)
- EPC rating and certificate data
- HMO licence information (if applicable)
- Email address (if found via Companies House or enrichment)
- Phone number (if found via enrichment)
- Mailing address (registered address or property address)

### Sources of data (Article 14 requirement)
We obtain landlord data from the following publicly available sources:
1. EPC Register — government database of Energy Performance Certificates (opendatacommunities.org)
2. Companies House — public register of UK companies and directors (companieshouse.gov.uk)
3. Land Registry CCOD — Corporate and Commercial Ownership Dataset (gov.uk)
4. Council HMO licensing registers — public registers of Houses in Multiple Occupation licences (local authority websites/FOI)
5. OpenRent — public property listing platform (openrent.com)

### Why we process your data (lawful basis)
Lawful basis: Legitimate interests — UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f)
Purpose: Direct marketing of property management services to landlords of rental properties in Greater Manchester
Full assessment: See our Legitimate Interest Assessment document

### Who we share data with
We do not share your personal data with third parties. Data is stored in a local database and used solely for SJB Property Group's direct marketing activities.

### How long we keep your data
We retain landlord lead data for 12 months from the date of collection. After 12 months, we review each record:
- If there has been no engagement or response, the data is deleted
- If there is an active business relationship or ongoing communication, data is retained for the duration of that relationship
See our Data Retention Policy for full details.

### Your rights
Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:
- **Access** your personal data (Subject Access Request)
- **Rectification** of inaccurate data
- **Erasure** of your data ("right to be forgotten")
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interests
- **Restriction** of processing
- **Data portability** (receive your data in a structured format)

To exercise any of these rights, contact: sam@sjbproperty.com

### How to opt out
- Email: Reply "STOP" to any marketing email
- Post: Return any direct mail with "opt out" written on the envelope
- Email us: sam@sjbproperty.com with subject "Opt Out"
Opt-outs are applied universally across all channels (email, post, phone, LinkedIn).

### Complaints
If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):
- Website: ico.org.uk
- Phone: 0303 123 1113
```

# Data Retention Policy — Landlord Lead Data
## SJB Property Group Ltd

### Scope
This policy applies to all personal data collected as part of SJB Property Group's landlord lead generation pipeline.

### Retention periods
| Data Category | Retention Period | Review Trigger |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Landlord lead data (name, address, EPC, contact) | 12 months from collection | No engagement after 12 months -> delete |
| Suppressed leads (opt-out records) | Indefinite | Suppression records must be retained permanently to prevent re-contacting |
| Business relationship data (signed clients) | Duration of relationship + 6 years | Legal/tax retention requirements |

### Review process
- Every 12 months, review all lead records with no engagement
- Delete records where: no email opened, no mail response, no phone contact, no consultation booked
- Retain records where: active communication exists, consultation scheduled or completed, management agreement in place

### Deletion process
- Lead records are deleted from the SQLite database
- Associated property records are retained (anonymised — no landlord link)
- Suppression records are NEVER deleted (to prevent re-contacting)
- Notion CRM entries are archived or deleted to match

### Document control
- Version: 1.0
- Date: {today}
- Next review: {today + 12 months}
```
