## Connection Request Templates

LinkedIn connection requests have a 300-character limit. Keep them short and personalised.

### Version A: Company Reference

> Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] owns rental properties in [area]. I run SJB Property Group -- we help landlords in Greater Manchester with hands-off property management. Would be great to connect.

### Version B: Property / EPC Reference

> Hi [Name], I help landlords in the [area] area manage their properties stress-free. With EPC regulations tightening, many landlords are looking for support. Happy to connect.

## Follow-up DM Sequence

Only send follow-up messages after the connection request has been accepted. Space messages out and stop if there is no reply after DM 3.

### Follow-up DM 1 (2-3 days after connection accepted)

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I noticed you own rental property in [area]. We manage 7 properties across Greater Manchester -- 100% rent collection, zero voids. If you ever want to take a step back from the day-to-day, I'd love to have a quick chat about how we could help. No pressure at all.

### Follow-up DM 2 (5-7 days after DM 1, if no reply)

> Hi [Name], just a quick follow-up. With EPC minimum standards tightening (your property at [address] is rated [EPC rating]), a lot of landlords are reviewing their options. We handle everything from compliance to tenant management for 10% of rent. Happy to jump on a 15-min call if useful -- just let me know.

### Follow-up DM 3 (7-10 days after DM 2, if no reply -- final message)

> Hi [Name], last message from me! If you ever need a hand with your rental properties in [area], here's my direct email: sam@sjbproperty.com. We're always happy to have an informal chat. All the best, Sam

## Best Practice Notes

- **Personalise every message** -- never send generic copy-paste. Reference something specific about their company, property, or area
- **Maximum 3 DMs if no reply** -- after DM 3, stop. Do not chase further
- **Respect LinkedIn's messaging limits** -- do not send bulk messages or use automation tools that violate LinkedIn's terms of service
- **Profile first** -- ensure your LinkedIn profile is up to date with SJB Property Group details, a professional photo, and relevant content before starting outreach
- **Engage with their content** -- if the landlord posts on LinkedIn, like or comment before sending a DM. It warms the conversation
- **Log outcomes** -- track connection acceptance rate, reply rate, and calls booked for each outreach batch
- **Opt-out** -- if anyone asks not to be contacted, respect it immediately and add them to the suppression list
