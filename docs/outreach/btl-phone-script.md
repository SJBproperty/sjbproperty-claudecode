# BTL Management -- Phone Script

**Type:** Warm follow-up referencing a previously sent letter
**Status:** Asset only -- no phone data currently available
**Author:** Sam Bradbury, SJB Property Group

---

## 1. Opening (Reference the Letter)

> "Hi, is that [Name]? This is Sam from SJB Property Group. I sent you a letter last week about your property at [property address]. I wanted to follow up and see if you had a chance to look at it."

**If gatekeeper answers:**

> "Hi, could I speak to [Name] please? It's Sam from SJB Property Group -- I wrote to them last week about their rental property."

**If voicemail:**

> "Hi [Name], this is Sam Bradbury from SJB Property Group. I sent you a letter recently about your property at [property address] and our property management services. If you'd like to have a quick chat, my number is [phone number] or you can email me at sam@sjbproperty.com. No pressure at all -- just wanted to introduce myself. Thanks!"

---

## 2. Discovery Questions (Identify Pain Points)

Use these open questions to understand their situation. Listen more than you talk.

- "How are you finding managing your rental property at the moment?"
- "Are you aware of the upcoming EPC regulation changes? Your property at [property address] currently has an [EPC rating] rating."
- "How much time would you say you spend each month dealing with tenant issues, maintenance, compliance?"
- "Have you experienced any void periods recently?"
- "Are you managing the property yourself, or do you have an agent?"

---

## 3. Pain Point Triggers (Respond to What They Say)

**If compliance stress:**

> "A lot of landlords are finding the regulatory burden overwhelming -- EPC minimum standards tightening to C by 2030, electrical safety checks, gas certificates, deposit protection rules... it's a lot to keep on top of, especially if you've got other commitments."

**If void costs:**

> "Empty properties cost on average GBP500-800 per month in lost rent plus ongoing bills. We currently manage 7 properties with zero void months in 2025. Keeping properties occupied and tenants happy is what we do."

**If time drain:**

> "Managing tenants, chasing rent, organising repairs -- it adds up. Our landlords tell us the biggest relief is just not having to think about it. They get their rent on time every month and we handle everything else."

**If tenant hassle:**

> "Difficult tenants are the number one reason landlords come to us. We handle everything -- from tenant screening to rent collection, maintenance coordination, and eviction proceedings if it ever comes to that."

---

## 4. Service Overview

> "We charge 10% of the monthly rent, fully inclusive. That covers tenant finding, rent collection, maintenance coordination, compliance management, regular inspections, and 24/7 emergency cover."

> "We currently manage 7 properties across Greater Manchester with a 100% rent collection record and zero void months."

> "For qualifying multi-occupancy properties, we also offer a guaranteed rent option -- fixed monthly income with zero voids, regardless of whether the property is occupied."

> "There's no long tie-in. You can leave with 2 months' notice if it's not working for you."

---

## 5. Objection Handling

### "I already have an agent"

> "That's great. How are you finding them? A lot of landlords come to us because their current agent isn't proactive enough on compliance or communication. We're a smaller, hands-on operation -- you deal directly with me, not a call centre."

### "10% is too expensive"

> "I understand. But consider the cost of a single void month, or a compliance fine -- those can run into thousands. Most of our landlords find the fee pays for itself in peace of mind and fewer problems. Plus there's no tie-in -- you can leave with 2 months' notice."

### "I'm thinking of selling"

> "Completely understand. If you do decide to hold, we're here. And actually, a well-managed property with reliable tenants in place fetches a better sale price than an empty one if you do decide to sell."

### "I manage fine myself"

> "That's fair. Many self-managing landlords do great. But if you ever find the regulations getting overwhelming or want your weekends back, give us a call. We're always happy to have an informal chat."

### "I only have one property"

> "That's absolutely fine. A lot of our clients have just one or two properties. The service is exactly the same regardless of portfolio size."

---

## 6. Close / Call to Action

**If interested:**

> "Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call next week where I can walk you through exactly what we'd do for your property? No obligation at all."

- If yes: book the call, confirm their email address for a calendar invite
- Confirm: "Great, I'll send you a calendar invite to [email]. Looking forward to speaking with you."

**If not right now:**

> "No problem at all. I'll send you an email with our details so you have them if anything changes. What's the best email for you?"

**If definite no:**

> "Completely understand. Thanks for your time, [Name]. If anything changes in the future, you know where to find us. All the best."

## Notes

- Always reference the letter and their specific property -- never cold call blind
- Listen more than you talk. The discovery questions are there to understand their pain, not to pitch
- If they mention compliance concerns, lean into the EPC angle -- it's the strongest hook
- Never pressure. The tone is helpful and consultative, not salesy
- Log every call outcome (interested / not now / no / voicemail / wrong number) for tracking
- If they ask to opt out, respect it immediately and add them to the suppression list
