### Part 1: Purpose Test

**What is the legitimate interest?**

Direct marketing of property management services to landlords of rental properties in Greater Manchester. SJB Property Group offers Buy-to-Let (BTL) management at 10% of monthly rent, and Rent-to-Rent (R2R) guaranteed rent agreements for House in Multiple Occupation (HMO) landlords.

**Who benefits from the processing?**

- **SJB Property Group** — business development, identifying landlords who may benefit from professional management
- **Landlords** — access to professional management services that reduce compliance burden, void periods, maintenance hassle, and regulatory risk (particularly around Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) minimum standards)

**Is the interest real and present?**

Yes. SJB Property Group has an active landlord lead pipeline with 9,545 records sourced from publicly available government registers and property listing platforms. The company currently manages 7 properties and is actively expanding its management portfolio.

**What is the lawful basis?**

UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests. The UK Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (in force from February 2026) explicitly confirms direct marketing as a legitimate interest. However, the full three-part test (purpose, necessity, balancing) remains a requirement, as direct marketing is not on the ICO's "recognised legitimate interests" list that would allow a simplified assessment.

---

### Part 2: Necessity Test

**Is the processing necessary for this purpose?**

Yes. To identify and contact landlords who may benefit from property management services, it is necessary to process their property ownership data and contact details. Without processing this data, there is no way to identify landlords of rental properties or communicate with them about relevant services.

**Is there a less intrusive alternative?**

No. The data sources used are already publicly available:

1. **EPC Register** — public government database of Energy Performance Certificates
2. **Companies House** — public register of UK companies and directors
3. **Land Registry CCOD** — Corporate and Commercial Ownership Dataset, publicly available
4. **Council HMO licensing registers** — public registers of HMO licences held by local authorities
5. **OpenRent** — public property listing platform

Processing publicly available data represents the least intrusive method of identifying landlords. The alternative — purchasing data from third-party brokers — would involve data with less transparency about its origins.

**What data do we minimally require?**

| Data Point | Why Needed |
|------------|-----------|
| Full name or company name | Identify and address the landlord |
| Entity type (Ltd, LLP, sole trader, individual) | PECR compliance — determines permitted contact channels |
| Company number (if applicable) | Verify corporate identity via Companies House |
| Property address(es) | Confirm rental property ownership; mailing address for direct mail |
| EPC rating and certificate data | Identify compliance risk (primary outreach hook) |
| HMO licence information | Identify HMO landlords for R2R service |
| Email address (corporate subscribers only) | Direct marketing via email (Ltd/LLP entities only) |
| Phone number (if available) | Follow-up contact channel |

No sensitive personal data (as defined by UK GDPR Article 9) is processed. No data about tenants, finances, health, or criminal records is collected.

### Part 3: Balancing Test

**Nature of the data**

Business contact information derived from publicly available government registers and property listing platforms. The data relates to individuals and companies in their capacity as commercial landlords — a business activity — not in their private or domestic capacity.

**Sources of data**

All data is sourced from publicly available registers:

1. **EPC Register** — government database at opendatacommunities.org
2. **Companies House** — public register at companieshouse.gov.uk
3. **Land Registry CCOD** — Corporate and Commercial Ownership Dataset at gov.uk
4. **Council HMO licensing registers** — public registers maintained by local authorities (accessed via FOI or published online)
5. **OpenRent** — public property listing platform at openrent.com

**Reasonable expectations**

Landlords who register properties on public government databases, file company information at Companies House, and list properties on letting platforms have a reasonable expectation of receiving business correspondence related to property services. Property management is directly relevant to their commercial activity as landlords.

**Relationship with data subjects**

There is no prior relationship — this is cold outreach. However, the communications are directly relevant to the recipient's role as a property landlord and concern services (property management, EPC compliance support) that relate to their existing business obligations.

**Impact on individuals**

Low. The processing involves:

- Business contact information only (not private or sensitive data)
- Communications about services directly relevant to their commercial activity
- Easy opt-out mechanisms on all channels
- No automated decision-making or profiling with legal effects

**Safeguards in place**

| Safeguard | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Opt-out on all channels | "Reply STOP" for email; return address on direct mail; email sam@sjbproperty.com |
| Universal suppression | Opt-out on any channel suppresses across ALL channels (email, post, phone, LinkedIn) |
| PECR compliance | Email marketing sent only to corporate subscribers (Ltd/LLP). Sole traders and individuals receive direct mail and phone contact only |
| Data retention review | 12-month retention period — records deleted if no engagement after 12 months |
| Secure storage | Data stored in local SQLite database; not shared with third parties |
| Data minimisation | Only data necessary for identifying and contacting landlords is processed |
| Access controls | Single-user system operated by the data controller |

**Children or vulnerable people**

Not applicable. The data relates exclusively to commercial landlords (companies and individuals acting in a business capacity). No data about children or vulnerable people is collected or processed.

**Right to object**

Landlords can object to processing at any time via:

- Email: sam@sjbproperty.com
- Reply "STOP" to any marketing email
- Return any direct mail with "opt out" written on the envelope

Objections result in immediate universal suppression across all contact channels. Suppression records are retained permanently to prevent re-contacting.

---

### Conclusion

The legitimate interest in direct marketing of property management services to landlords outweighs the minimal privacy impact, given that:

1. Data is sourced entirely from public registers and platforms
2. Communications are directly relevant to the recipient's commercial activity as a landlord
3. Robust opt-out and universal suppression mechanisms are in place
4. PECR compliance is enforced through entity type gating (email only to corporate subscribers)
5. A 12-month data retention review ensures records are not held indefinitely without engagement
6. No sensitive personal data is processed

The processing may proceed on the basis of legitimate interests under UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f).
